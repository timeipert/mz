The Transcription Editor

The transcription editor is the core of Monodi-Zero. It is designed around keyboard efficiency, allowing you to transcribe manuscript staves quickly and accurately without constantly switching to a mouse.

Basic Concepts & Structure

Transcription in Monodi-Zero follows a logical, hierarchical block model. Rather than working with unstructured text, you manipulate distinct musical objects:

  • Folio Changes: Page breaks in the source manuscript (e.g. 1r, 2v). Loading a folio automatically triggers the IIIF viewer to load that folio's manuscript page.
  • Line Changes: Physical staff lines in the source. This is the unit used to link transcriptions directly to drawn zones in the IIIF image editor.
  • Clefs: Anchors defining the pitch scale for the staff lines. Note pitches on the staff are automatically computed relative to the active clef.
  • Syllables: Structural containers holding both the lyric text and one or more notes (neumes).

Clefs & Staff Lines

Chant notation in Monodi-Zero uses a standard 5-line staff representation. To establish the pitch space, you must place a clef at the start of a staff line:

  • C Clef (Do): Indicates that whichever staff line it sits on represents the pitch C (Do). Can be positioned on lines 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5.
  • F Clef (Fa): Indicates that whichever staff line it sits on represents the pitch F (Fa). Can be positioned on lines 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5.

Any notes positioned after a clef will dynamically compute their musical pitch relative to it. If you shift the clef up or down, all subsequent notes automatically adjust their staff position to maintain the correct auditory pitch.

Scholarly Note Shapes & Ornaments

Monodi-Zero supports several standard chant neume types to ensure scholarly accuracy when transcribing medieval manuscripts. You can toggle these shapes using keyboard keys:

  • Normal Punctum (n): The standard square/lozenge note. Used for regular, plain chant notes.
  • Liquescent (l): Renders as a smaller note symbol with a descending hook. Used to transcribe syllables containing diphthongs (e.g., au, ei) or liquid/nasal consonants (e.g., l, m, n, r), indicating a vocal slide or softened articulation.
  • Oriscus (o): Renders as a wavy lozenge. Indicates an ornamental, auxiliary pitch or a vocal tension on the note.
  • Quilisma (q): Renders as a jagged, toothed diamond. Denotes a passing note with a light vocal tremolo, usually leading upward to a main accent note.

Transcription Workflow

1. Syllable and Lyric Editing

Place your cursor in the syllable lyric row (using arrow keys or clicking). Type the lyric text. Pressing Space splits the lyric, completes the current syllable, and creates a new syllable block to the right. Pressing Backspace on empty text merges the syllable back into the previous one.

2. Note Navigation & Pitch Tuning

Press Arrow Up to shift focus from the syllable lyric text directly to the notes staff above it. Press Space to insert a note, and use Arrow Up or Arrow Down to adjust the note's pitch vertically. Pressing l, o, or q changes its type.

The Editor Sidebar

The collapsible sidebar on the left of the editor has three tabs:

  • Meta: The document's metadata fields — see Metadata & Export.
  • Struct: The structural outline of the chant (see below).
  • Comments: All critical-apparatus comments of the document as colour-coded cards — see Critical Apparatus.

Organizing Sections (Struct tab)

Longer chants are organized into named sections (e.g. strophes, verses, or antiphon/verse divisions). The Struct tab gives you a live outline of the document:

  • Structural layers: Choose how many nesting levels (0–3) the document hierarchy uses. The labels of these levels can follow your genre profiles configured in Settings.
  • Hierarchy tree: Every section, staff line, and paratext appears as a row, indented by depth. Section names are edited inline — the name is stored as the section's signature and appears in PDF exports and the synopsis.
  • Restructuring (hover a row to reveal the actions):
    • Split — cut a section in two at any line or paratext.
    • Merge — join a section with the following one.
    • Delete — remove an empty section.

Paratexts

Besides music, sources contain paratext — rubrics, incipit references, performance instructions. Insert a paratext block via the block toolbar (+ Text), type its text, and classify its type. Paratexts appear inline in the transcription, in the synopsis, and in PDF exports.

Keyboard Shortcuts Cheat Sheet

Category Shortcut Action
Navigation ← / → Move cursor between Syllables, Notes, Clefs, or Lines.
↑ Shift focus from Syllable lyric text to the Notes above.
↓ Shift focus from Notes back to the Syllable lyric text below.
Note Input Space (on Staff) Inserts a new note at the current active position.
↑ / ↓ (on Note) Alter the pitch of the currently focused note up/down on the staff.
n / l / o / q Set note type: Normal, Liquescent, Oriscus, or Quilisma.
Backspace / Del Deletes the selected note from the staff.
c Inserts a new Clef at the cursor position.
Text & Structure Space (in Text) Splits lyrics and moves to the next syllable block.
Enter (in Text) Creates a new Line Change block below.
i / j Insert structural line block before (i) or after (j) the active element.
