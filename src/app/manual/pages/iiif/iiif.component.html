IIIF Integration

Monodi-Zero integrates directly with the International Image Interoperability Framework (IIIF) to provide side-by-side comparison of manuscript images and your digital transcriptions.

View Modes

You can toggle your view mode from the main toolbar. Hover over the options below to preview their behavior on the mock layout on the right:

  • Split Screen Mode: Displays the transcription editor on the left and the IIIF manuscript viewer on the right, perfect for mapping and reference.
  • Transcription Mode: Maximizes the keyboard editor, hiding the IIIF panel for distraction-free data entry.
  • IIIF Mode: Maximizes the high-resolution image viewer, hiding the editor to inspect details.

Linking Regions (Line to Zone Mapping)

When in Split Screen mode, you can map specific transcription lines directly to the corresponding stave region on the manuscript image. Hover over the trigger below to visualize this connection:

  1. Draw a Zone: In the IIIF viewer, draw an annotation box over a line of music on the manuscript image.
  2. Establish a Link: Hover over Link Stave to Image to see how the transcription block connects to the manuscript. Click the Link button on the drawn zone and select the corresponding Line Change in the editor.

Once linked, clicking a manuscript zone will scroll the editor to that line. Clicking a staff line will center and zoom the IIIF viewer to its mapped zone. Click below to test it:

Automatic Folio Navigation

When transcribing, inserting or navigating past a Folio Change ([F]) block will instruct the IIIF viewer to fetch and render the new manuscript page automatically, maintaining synchronization as you work.
