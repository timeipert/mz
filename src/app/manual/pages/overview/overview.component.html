Overview: What can you do with Monodi-Zero?

Monodi-Zero is a state-of-the-art web application designed for the digital transcription, cataloging, and analysis of historical musical sources—specifically medieval chants in square and neume notation. It brings together a keyboard-driven notation editor, a IIIF manuscript image viewer, an advanced melodic/text search engine, and a critical apparatus into a single cohesive research environment.

Logical Data Structure

To understand how to navigate and use Monodi-Zero, it is helpful to understand how information is structured hierarchically:

  • Sources: The top-level containers representing a physical manuscript or collection (e.g., a specific codex).
  • Documents: Individual musical pieces, chants, or sections within a Source (e.g., an Introit or Alleluia).
  • Sections: Structural partitions within a Document (e.g., different parts of a chant).
  • Folios & Lines: Physical references mapping the text directly to the source manuscript page (Folio) and line number.
  • Syllables & Notes: The core music-text bond. Notes are bound directly to their corresponding textual syllable to preserve the alignment of chant lyrics and melody.

Key Features

  1. Transcription Editor: A specialized editor designed around a "keyboard-first" workflow. You can input syllables, pitches, clefs, and custom neume shapes without touching your mouse, enabling extremely high-speed data entry. A structure sidebar lets you organize chants into named sections with up to three nesting levels.
  2. Critical Apparatus (Complex Comments): Document melodic and textual variants across different parallel manuscripts. Comments are attached to precise note ranges via a guided two-step selection, shown as colour-coded brackets, and structured as a recursive tree (grids and leaves) to align version differences side-by-side.
  3. Melodic & Textual Search: Four search tabs — full-text, source metadata, document metadata, and melody. Full-text search offers phrase / all-words / any-word / fuzzy modes with medieval spelling tolerance; melody search supports absolute pitches, intervals, and contour with adjustable Levenshtein distance. Every result list can be filtered further after the search.
  4. Analysis & Statistics: Compare witnesses side-by-side in the Synoptic Comparison (four alignment strategies, consensus text, vector PDF export), discover recurring formulas with the Melodic Formula Analysis (background computation, timeline overview, permalinks), and study pattern distribution per source on the Stats page.
  5. IIIF Manuscript Integration: Link your transcription line-by-line to digitized high-resolution manuscript pages served via the International Image Interoperability Framework (IIIF). Draw zones and link them to scroll in sync.
  6. Academic Exports: Export transcriptions to standard MEI (Music Encoding Initiative) XML, publication-ready vector PDFs (single documents and synopses), or a self-contained offline HTML edition with built-in search.
  7. Local-first Workspace: All data lives in your browser — no server, no account. Import existing corpora from ZIP archives (monodi and OMMR4all layouts), back up or share via workspace files or GitHub synchronization, and maintain storage health from the Workspace settings tab.
