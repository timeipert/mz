Search Functionality

The Search page is the central hub for querying your corpus. It offers four search tabs, post-search filtering, and is the entry point for the Synoptic Comparison and the Melodic Formula Analysis (see Analysis & Statistics).

The Four Search Tabs

  • Quick: Full-text search across all lyric texts and metadata. Four matching modes are available: Phrase (exact word sequence), All words, Any word, and Fuzzy (with an adjustable Levenshtein distance). The medieval spelling tolerance toggle normalizes common orthographic variance (e.g. u/v, i/j, ae/e) so iustus also finds justus. Matches are shown with a highlighted text snippet.
  • Sources: Structured metadata query over source fields — siglum, dating, region/place/institution of origin, order tradition, source type, library, and shelfmark.
  • Documents: Structured metadata query over document fields — document ID, incipit, genres, feast, commentary, and more.
  • Melody: Searches melodic content. Enter pitch names (e.g. f g a), intervals, or a contour. Options include octave sensitivity, restricting matches to stay within one syllable, and a maximum Levenshtein distance for fuzzy melodic matching. Results show the matching passage rendered on a staff with the matched syllables highlighted.

Searches run only when you click Search — nothing runs automatically. Long scans over large corpora show live progress and can be cancelled; results stream in as they are found. Your search-mode preferences (matching mode, tolerances) are remembered between sessions, but each visit starts with a clean form.

Filtering & Result Tables

  • Filter bar: Every result list has a filter box to narrow the current results without re-searching. Multiple words are combined with AND.
  • Configurable columns: Source and document result tables have a column picker; your column choices are remembered.
  • Sortable headers and pagination keep even thousands of results manageable.

Fuzzy Matching (Levenshtein Distance)

Chant manuscripts frequently contain scribal variants, spelling differences, or slight musical variations. Both the Quick tab (fuzzy mode) and the Melody tab include an adjustable Levenshtein Distance that allows insertions, deletions, and substitutions up to your threshold:

  • Distance = 0: Exact matches only.
  • Distance = 1: Allows for exactly one deviation. For example, a search for A B C will match A D C (substitution) or A B (deletion of C).

Navigating Results

Clicking a result opens the corresponding source or document; melody results scroll directly to the matching line with the matched syllables highlighted for immediate identification.

From Search to Analysis

  • Compare: Tick the checkboxes of two or more documents in any result list — a floating bar appears; click Compare to open the Synoptic Comparison.
  • Pattern Analysis: The Melodic Formula Analysis always operates on your current result set, so search first to scope it (e.g. one genre or one source), then open the analysis.
