Search Functionality

Monodi-Zero features a highly robust search engine tailored for musicologists. It allows you to search both textual lyrics and melodic sequences across all documents in your project.

Search Modes & Capabilities

The search engine operates in three primary modes depending on your toggles:

  • Text Search: Searches the lyrical text (syllables) of all transcriptions. It matches words or partial words instantly as you type.
  • Absolute Pitch Search: Activated by toggling the music icon. You type pitch names (e.g., a b c or f g a) to find exact melodic segments. Pitches are case-insensitive.
  • Interval (Contour) Search: Activated by toggling the steps icon. This searches for relative melodic intervals rather than absolute pitches. For example, searching for a step-up followed by a step-down (expressed as +2 -2) matches any melody with that contour, regardless of what pitch it starts on. This is extremely powerful for identifying transposed versions of the same chant.

Fuzzy Matching (Levenshtein Distance)

Chant manuscripts frequently contain scribal variants, spelling differences, or slight musical variations. To accommodate this, Monodi-Zero includes an adjustable Levenshtein Distance slider that allows insertions, deletions, and substitutions up to your threshold:

  • Distance = 0: Exact matches only.
  • Distance = 1: Allows for exactly one deviation. For example, a search for A B C will match A D C (substitution) or A B (deletion of C).

Navigating Results

Clicking on any result in the search panel will automatically open the corresponding source document and scroll directly to the matching line, highlighting the matched syllables or notes in yellow for immediate identification.
